# Outputs Description

This document explains the figures and videos generated by the Formula 1 analysis pipeline. Static figures are stored in `outputs/figures/`; videos are stored in `outputs/videos/`.

## Static Figures

All report-oriented static figures exclude 2026 data. Modern figures use 2019-2025 data, while historical figures use the SQLite historical dataset.

### Dataset and Historical Background

#### [`yearly_records_2019_2025.png`](figures/yearly_records_2019_2025.png)

![Modern Dataset Size by Season](figures/yearly_records_2019_2025.png)

Shows the number of driver-race records and race count in each modern season from 2019 to 2025. It explains the scale and year coverage of the modern analysis dataset.

Use in report: dataset description.

#### [`historical_races_by_year.png`](figures/historical_races_by_year.png)

![Formula 1 Race Count by Year](figures/historical_races_by_year.png)

Shows how the Formula 1 calendar expanded from 1950 to 2018. It provides historical background and shows that the sport has grown from a small calendar to a larger global championship.

Use in report: case background.

#### [`historical_top10_driver_wins.png`](figures/historical_top10_driver_wins.png)

![Historical Top 10 Driver Wins](figures/historical_top10_driver_wins.png)

Ranks the top 10 race-winning drivers in the historical SQLite data. It identifies historically dominant drivers such as Michael Schumacher, Lewis Hamilton, Alain Prost, Sebastian Vettel, and Ayrton Senna.

Use in report: case background and historical context.

#### [`historical_top10_constructor_wins.png`](figures/historical_top10_constructor_wins.png)

![Historical Top 10 Constructor Wins](figures/historical_top10_constructor_wins.png)

Ranks the top 10 race-winning constructors in the historical SQLite data. It highlights long-term constructor dominance, especially Ferrari, McLaren, Williams, Mercedes, and Red Bull.

Use in report: case background and historical context.

## Grid, Qualifying, and Race Result Analysis

#### [`grid_position_outcome_rates.png`](figures/grid_position_outcome_rates.png)

![Race Outcome Rates by Grid Position](figures/grid_position_outcome_rates.png)

Compares win rate, podium rate, and top-10 rate by starting grid position for 2019-2025. It shows that better starting positions generally lead to better race outcomes.

Use in report: analysis of starting grid influence.

#### [`qualifying_position_outcome_rates.png`](figures/qualifying_position_outcome_rates.png)

![Qualifying Position and Race Outcome](figures/qualifying_position_outcome_rates.png)

Shows how qualifying position relates to podium rate, top-10 rate, and average finish position. It demonstrates the predictive value of qualifying performance.

Use in report: analysis of qualifying influence.

#### [`qualifying_finish_heatmap_2019_2025.png`](figures/qualifying_finish_heatmap_2019_2025.png)

![Qualifying Position vs Finish Position Heatmap](figures/qualifying_finish_heatmap_2019_2025.png)

Displays the joint distribution of qualifying position and finish position. Darker cells indicate more frequent combinations. This is more informative than a simple average because it shows the full distribution of race outcomes.

Use in report: technical analysis of qualifying-to-race relationship.

#### [`grid_advantage_historical_vs_modern.png`](figures/grid_advantage_historical_vs_modern.png)

![Grid Advantage: Historical vs Modern Era](figures/grid_advantage_historical_vs_modern.png)

Compares historical and modern grid advantage using pole win rate, front-3 podium rate, and front-10 top-10 rate. It shows whether starting position has become more or less important in the modern era.

Use in report: historical-modern comparison.

#### [`position_change_distribution_2019_2025.png`](figures/position_change_distribution_2019_2025.png)

![Race Position Change Distribution](figures/position_change_distribution_2019_2025.png)

Shows how often drivers gained or lost positions relative to their grid starts. Positive values mean a driver finished ahead of the starting position.

Use in report: race position-change analysis.

## Advanced Analytical Indicators

#### [`feature_correlation_heatmap_2019_2025.png`](figures/feature_correlation_heatmap_2019_2025.png)

![Feature Correlation Heatmap](figures/feature_correlation_heatmap_2019_2025.png)

Shows correlations between pre-race features and target outcomes such as finish position, podium, and top-10 finish. It supports the later prediction task by showing which features contain useful information.

Use in report: feature analysis and modeling motivation.

#### [`constructor_competitiveness_by_year_2019_2025.png`](figures/constructor_competitiveness_by_year_2019_2025.png)

![Constructor Points Concentration by Season](figures/constructor_competitiveness_by_year_2019_2025.png)

Shows the share of total constructor points captured by the top 1, top 2, and top 3 teams each season. It measures how concentrated the competition is.

Use in report: competitive balance analysis.

#### [`constructor_hhi_competitiveness_2019_2025.png`](figures/constructor_hhi_competitiveness_2019_2025.png)

![Constructor Championship Concentration Index](figures/constructor_hhi_competitiveness_2019_2025.png)

Uses the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) and effective constructor count to measure competitive concentration. A higher HHI means points are more concentrated among fewer teams.

Use in report: professional competition concentration metric.

#### [`pre_race_strength_podium_rate_2019_2025.png`](figures/pre_race_strength_podium_rate_2019_2025.png)

![Pre-Race Strength and Podium Rate](figures/pre_race_strength_podium_rate_2019_2025.png)

Shows podium rate by pre-race strength bins, such as driver pre-race rank and recent average points. It checks whether pre-race information can separate high-probability podium candidates from lower-probability drivers.

Use in report: pre-race feature effectiveness analysis.

#### [`status_mix_by_year_2019_2025.png`](figures/status_mix_by_year_2019_2025.png)

![Race Result Status Mix by Season](figures/status_mix_by_year_2019_2025.png)

Shows the share of finished, lapped, and other race statuses by season. It provides a basic reliability and race-completion view.

Use in report: race status and reliability analysis.

## Videos

Videos are tracked in Git for direct report and presentation use. They can also be regenerated by running `python animate_f1_points.py`.

#### [`constructor_points_race_progress_1950_2025.mp4`](videos/constructor_points_race_progress_1950_2025.mp4)

Animated bar chart showing constructor season cumulative points after each race from 1950 to 2025. Points reset at the start of each season, matching championship rules.

Use in report or presentation: dynamic team standings evolution.

#### [`constructor_all_time_points_progress_1950_2025.mp4`](videos/constructor_all_time_points_progress_1950_2025.mp4)

Animated bar chart showing all-time constructor cumulative points from 1950 to 2025. Points do not reset between seasons. This video shows long-term constructor influence and historical accumulation.

Use in report or presentation: long-term constructor dominance.

#### [`driver_points_race_progress_2019_2025.mp4`](videos/driver_points_race_progress_2019_2025.mp4)

Animated bar chart showing driver season cumulative points after each race from 2019 to 2025. Points reset at the start of each season.

Use in report or presentation: modern driver standings evolution.
